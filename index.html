About Me

Hey! I'm Evan, I was born in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, and lived here (almost) all my life. I love programming. Be it in HTML and CSS like this page, or various back-end languages like java, python, and C#, it's what I find most interesting. I found my passion playing video games in 2012, when everything about computers just absolutely fascinated me. I knew I wanted to do something in the realm of computer technology.

Whether it was games like Minecraft, random inspect tabs on my browser, or a conglomeration of various YouTube videos, I found myself learning a lot about how computers and development worked. I went into it without really understanding a path of development I would like to follow. So on goes my middleschool and highschool educational journey.

Education

My middleschool education wasn't really filled with much learning in classes about my technical aspirations, though it was home to a lot of my hobby learning and where my interest really grew. From many different browser based coding environments, trying to create my own games, my journey started here, in a base level game development class.

Fast forward to highschool, this is where my learning really began in a formal sense. At Southmoore Highschool, they offered a couple different classes a freshman like me could look forward to trying out. I started with HTML and Web Design, learning along with the curriculum. It was fairly entry level which I got bored with quickly, so in the time between my assignments in class I typically looked to google to self teach javascript to spruce up the pages I would make along the way.

I would then go on to learn and compete in various SWOSU SWIM meets for both HTML and Java. winning first place for web design and second for java, ahead of many other highschool competitors; however, that brings an end to my highschool programming career.

After graduation I enrolled in the wonderful vocational school Moore Norman Technology Center where I studied Java, C#, Visual Basic, and Python in a much more in depth classroom setting. Competing in Business Professionals of America (BPA) competitions for each, having won first place for both C# and Python at state my second year attending. After the two year course I graduated from the institution as an honors student of the National Technical Honors Society (NTHS).
